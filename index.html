Antarctica is the coldest, windiest, and driest place on Earth and has the highest average elevation of all continents. Its surface is 97-99% covered in ice, making it the largest body of ice in the world.

Where is it?

Antarctica is approximately centered at the Geographic South Pole and is surrounded by the Southern Ocean. It is 1.5x larger than the United States. For several thousand dollars, you can cruise on over to the massive ice sheet from Chile, Argentina, South Africa, or Australia.

Who Owns It?

Antarctica is not owned by anyone. There are no indigenous people nor permanent residents in Antarctica. However, there are tourists and over 40 permanent reserch stations built by many different countries where scientists stay to work.

Who cares?

Antarctica is important for its natural resources, as a venue for international collaboration in the scientific community, and as a driver of global climate. Only 3% of all water on Earth is fresh, and 90% of this fresh water is stored in Antarctica. Plus, Antarctica is home to many animals. Like penguins!
