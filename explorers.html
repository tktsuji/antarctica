Antarctic Explorers

Timeline

  • Jan. 1773

    Captain James Cook of Britain becomes the first person to circumnavigate Antarctica.

  • Jan. 1820

    Fabien Gottlieb von Bellingshausen of Russia becomes the first person to sight Antarctica.

  • Nov. 1820

    Nathaniel Brown Palmer becomes the first American to sight Antarctica.

  • Feb. 1822

    James Weddell of Britain breaks through the ice packs of the Weddell Sea and makes it the farthest South at the time.

  • Jan. 1841

    James Clark Ross of Britain enters the Ross Sea and discovers the Transantarctic Mountains, Mount Erebus, and the Ross Ice Shelf.

  • Jan. 1895

    Henryk Johan Bull of Norway lands at Cape Adare and arguably becomes the first person on the Antarctic Mainland.

  • Dec. 1911

    Roald Amundsen of Norway defeats Scott in a race to the South Pole.

  • Jan. 1912

    Robert Scott reaches the South Pole but dies with his crew on the way back.

  • Dec. 1938

    Schwabenland Expedition begins: Airships map 600,000 square kilometers of land and take 11,000 photographs.

  • 1946

    Operation Highjump: As a military exercise, U.S. troops map parts of Antarctica and discover Bunger Hills.

  • 1949-1952

    Maudheim Expedition: An international science expedition establishes a research station.

  • 1955-1959

    Deepfreeze Operations: The USA establishes research stations, and men winter over to conduct research.

  • 1957-1958

    International Geophysical Year: Scientists from 67 countries participate in research, 50 research stations are established, and the framework for later international negotiations are established.
